    Well done! An intervals is a continuum of real numbers. The interval [1,2] is all real numbers starting at 1 and ending at 2. It contains infinitely many real numbers, among them 1.1, 1.2, 1.21, 1.75, pi/2, the square roots of 2 and 3. Every real number with a decimal expansion that starts with 1. is in this set.

    The set with just the numbers 1 and 2 in it is written {1,2}. You must be careful to distinguish {} and [].

    Well done. An interval is a continuum of real numbers, not a set of consecutive integers. (2,5) contains infinitely many numbers that are not in [3,4], among them, 2.4 and 4.6. [3,4] is therefore a proper subset of (2,5).
